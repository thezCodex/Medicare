FIRST AID

First aid is the immediate and temporary care given to a person who has been injured or is suddenly ill. This care is provided until professional medical help can be obtained or the person recovers.

FIRST AID GUIDE

Essential steps to handle common emergencies.

Bleeding

Minor Cuts and Scraps:

  • Clean the wound with water
  • Apply an antiseptic.
  • Cover with a sterile bandage or dressing.

Sever Bleeding:

  • Apply direct pressure with a clean cloth or bandage.
  • Elevate the injured area if possible
  • Seek emergency medical help.

Burns

Minor burns

  • cool the burn under running water
  • cover with a sterile, non-stick dressing
  • Cover with a sterile bandage or dressing.
  • Avoid applying creams or ointments unless prescribed by a doctor

Severe burns:

  • Give 5 back blows between the infant's shoulder blades.
  • Avoid removing clothing stuck to the burn
  • Seek emergency medical help.

Choking

Conscious Adult or Child (over 1 year)

  • Perform the Heimlich maneuver (abdominal thrusts).
  • If the person cannot cough, speak, or breathe, continue thrusts until the objectis expelled or the person becomes unconscious.

Infant (under 1 year):

  • Do not immerse in water; instead, cover with a clean, cool, moist cloth
  • Follow with 5 chest thrusts (compressions)

Fractures

  • Immobilize the injured area
  • Do not attempt to realign the bone.
  • Use a splint or padding to immobilize the fracture.
  • Apply a cold pack to reduce swelling.
  • Seek medical attention.

Heart Attack

Minor Cuts and Scraps:

  • Have the person sit down and stay calm
  • If trained and advised, give aspirin
  • If the person becomes unresponsive, begin CPR.

Poisoning

if the person is conscious

  • Call the poison control center immediately
  • Do not induce vomiting unless instructed.
  • Identify the poison if possible.

if the person is unconscious

  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Begin CPR if the person is not breathing

Shock

Minor Cuts and Scraps:

  • Lay the person down and elevate their legs
  • Do not attempt to realign the bone.
  • Keep the person warm and comfortable. the fracture
  • Seek emergency medical help
